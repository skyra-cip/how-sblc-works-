# how-sblc-works-
A Standby Letter of Credit (SBLC) is one of the most widely used payment security instruments in international commodity trading.
It provides assurance that a seller may receive payment if a buyer fails to meet contractual obligations.

# Why Commodity Traders Use SBLC

SBLCs are commonly used in:

EN590 Diesel Transactions
Jet A1 Fuel Contracts
LNG Deals
Crude Oil Trading
Large Commodity Purchases

# Key Benefits
Reduces counterparty risk
Improves trust between buyer and seller
Facilitates international transactions
Supports trade finance structures

# Typical SBLC Process
Buyer signs SPA
Issuing bank issues SBLC
Advising bank verifies instrument
Seller accepts SBLC
Shipment proceeds
Seller may draw under SBLC if buyer defaults
